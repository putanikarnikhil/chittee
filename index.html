How Do Chits Work?

 

A simple, transparent process for collective saving and borrowing.

 
 
   
 
   
 

1. Form a Group

 

A group of members agrees on a chit value (e.g., ₹5,00,000) and duration (e.g., 50 months).

 
   
 
   
 

2. Contribute

 

Each member contributes a fixed amount monthly (e.g., ₹10,000) to create a central 'pot'.

 
   
 
   
 

3. Auction the Pot

 

The pot is auctioned. The member willing to take the lowest amount (highest 'discount') wins the pot for that month.

 
   
 
   
 

4. Distribute Dividend

 

The discount amount is distributed equally as a 'dividend' among all other members, reducing their next month's contribution.

 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 

A Financial Lifeline

 

Why Chit Funds are a Boon for SMEs

 

  Chit funds bridge the gap between expensive informal loans and inaccessible formal banking, providing a unique financial solution for small enterprises.  

 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 

Easy Access to Capital

 
 
  Provides quick access to funds for working capital, business expansion, or emergencies without the stringent procedures of banks.  
 
 
 
 
   
 

Minimal Documentation

 
 
  Unlike banks, chit funds often require minimal paperwork, making them suitable for small businesses with less formal accounting.  
 
 
 
 
   
 

Flexible \& Profitable Savings

 
 
  Allows businesses to invest surplus cash and earn returns through dividends. Daily contribution schemes are often available to match cash flow cycles.  
 
 
 
 
   
 

Market-Determined Interest

 
 
  The interest rate (the auction discount) is determined by the members' demand, creating a fair, market-driven borrowing cost.  
 
 
 
 
 
     
 
 
 

Regulations \& Auctions

 

Ensuring transparency, accountability, and fair play in every chit scheme.

 
 
 
 

Registration \& Regulation

 
     
  •   \✓   Schemes are registered with the Registrar of Chits, ensuring legal oversight.  
    •  
  •   \✓   The chit manager must deposit 100% of the chit value with the Registrar as security.  
    •  
  •   \✓   A government-mandated cap (e.g., 40% of chit value) on bidding prevents predatory rates.  
    •  
  •   \✓   Regular audits and filing of auction minutes ensure transparency and boost member confidence.  
    •  
 
 
 

The Auction Process

 
     
  •   \✓   Members assemble monthly to bid on the 'pot'. The auction is swift, typically lasting five minutes.  
    •  
  •   \✓   The highest bidder (one who offers the highest discount) wins the pot.  
    •  
  •   \✓   Members unable to attend can submit sealed bids in advance.  
    •  
  •   \✓   In case of no bidders, a lottery is conducted among non-prized members at a minimum discount.  
    •  
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 

Research \& Preliminary Findings

 

Data shows that chit funds offer competitive loan rates and follow distinct bidding patterns.

 
 
 

Loan Interest Rate Patterns

 

Analysis of bidding data from Thiripura Chits reveals that loan interest rates tend to follow a bell-shaped curve. Rates increase midway through the scheme's duration and decrease towards the end as the number of members needing funds reduces. This pattern suggests that bidding is driven by immediate need rather than pre-calculated interest rates.

Glossary of Terms

 

Key terms to help you understand the world of chit funds.

 
 
 
 

Auction

 

A procedure to identify the member who will receive the chit amount by bidding the highest permissible discount.

 
 
 

Contribution

 

The fixed amount payable by each member every month into the 'pot'.

 
 
 

Discount

 

The difference between the total chit value and the amount the winning bidder agrees to take. This is effectively the interest paid by the borrower.

 
 
 

Dividend

 

The 'discount' amount, minus the foreman's commission, which is distributed equally among all non-prized members.
